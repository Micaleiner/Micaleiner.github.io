About me

About me

I was born and lived in sunny South Africa until 2014 when my husband and I permanently settled in the beautiful city of Stuttgart, Germany. I have been a member of the Württembergischer Art Association since then.

From a very young age I loved creating things and naturally picked art as a subject at school, and enrolled for a BA degree in Fine art. After completing the first year I switched my studies to fashion designing and received my diploma. I started working as designer and eventually owned my own fashion boutique. I always took big mental leaps and started new things, so that was just a beginning of a very exiting journey that took me to wonderful places, many changes and plenty opportunities.

The song ‘she's a free and gentle flower growing wild’ relates to me, as I feel deeply and sensitive to others, and are totally addicted to the thrill of creating. I like to put motion and meaning into my work rather than reality.

The word ‘diverse’ truly describes my art. My subjects can be anything, from portraits, cityscapes, wild life, flowers as well as the abstract. The subject matter of each piece of work spontaneously determines the materials and the style. I love oil and acrylic paint as a basis but also enjoy waterpaint from time to time. Structure paste, gold leaf and other collage items are often incorporated for the more modern work. The painting seems to come alive with the use of thin, yet visible brush or palette knife strokes, and emphasis are placed on the accurate depiction of light in its changing qualities.

Different techniques and styles are used that range from impressionistic, idealistic, expressionistic and abstract. Colours are also incorporated to portray ‘feelings’ - bright colors for happiness, darker colors for sadness. The abstract work is normally a planned composition that is rational and calm, however the excitement comes from the tension set up between the colors and shapes.
